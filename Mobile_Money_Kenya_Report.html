01  ·  Background

From Cash to Clicks: Kenya's Financial Revolution

In 2007, Safaricom launched M-Pesa — a simple SMS-based money transfer service — with a modest goal: help unbanked Kenyans send money home. Seventeen years later, it handles more transactions than Visa does in sub-Saharan Africa.

The numbers are extraordinary. By 2024, mobile money accounts outnumber bank accounts by more than 6 to 1. The total value of mobile money transactions has grown to exceed 50% of Kenya's GDP — a penetration rate unmatched anywhere in the world. What began as a tool for the unbanked has become the backbone of commerce, government services, and financial inclusion.

This report analyses transaction volume, user growth, regional adoption, and the competitive landscape to surface the key trends shaping Kenya's mobile money market through 2024.

02  ·  Transaction Volume Trends

18 Years of Exponential Growth

Transaction volumes have grown at a compound annual rate of over 28% since 2010, with brief dips in 2020 (COVID-19 lockdowns) followed by accelerated recovery as government stimulus payments flowed through mobile money rails.

Annual Mobile Money Transaction Value — Kenya (KES Billions, 2015–2024)
Source: Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) Payment System Report, 2024

Key Insight: The 2020–2021 period saw a 34% spike in transaction value despite lower volumes. This reflects the government's decision to waive M-Pesa transaction fees during COVID-19, pushing higher-value transfers onto mobile rails for the first time.

03  ·  Active User Growth

Who Uses Mobile Money?

Active mobile money users grew from 12 million in 2015 to over 38 million in 2024. Growth has been particularly strong among women (now 46% of users, up from 32% in 2015) and rural users (48% of active accounts).

Active Mobile Money Users — Kenya (Millions, 2015–2024)
Source: Communications Authority of Kenya, Q4 2024 Sector Statistics Report

Gender Gap Closing: Female participation rose from 32% (2015) to 46% (2024), driven by targeted financial literacy campaigns and the Hustler Fund's mobile-first disbursement model.

04  ·  Market Structure

A Dominant Player, A Competitive Fringe

M-Pesa (Safaricom) commands approximately 96% market share by transaction value, making it one of the most dominant fintech platforms anywhere in the world. Airtel Money and Telkom's T-Kash together hold the remaining 4%.

Mobile Money Market Share by Transaction Value (2024)
Source: Communications Authority of Kenya, 2024

Regulatory Note: The CBK has been pushing for interoperability between mobile money platforms since 2022. Full interoperability could unlock a new wave of competition, particularly in merchant payments and cross-border remittances.

06  ·  Regional Analysis

Urban vs Rural: Bridging the Divide

Nairobi and Mombasa account for 52% of transaction value despite holding only 18% of Kenya's population — reflecting higher average transaction sizes in urban areas. However, rural transaction volume has grown at nearly double the urban rate since 2020.

Mobile Money Transaction Value by Region — 2024 (KES Billions)
Source: Safaricom Annual Report 2024 & CBK Regional Data

Digital Divide Alert: North Eastern Kenya (Mandera, Wajir, Garissa) has the lowest mobile money penetration at 38% — compared to 94% in Nairobi. Poor network coverage and low smartphone penetration remain key barriers.

07  ·  Recommendations

What the Data Tells Us to Do Next

Based on this analysis, here are four evidence-based recommendations for stakeholders across the mobile money ecosystem.

🔴 High Priority
Accelerate Rural Network Infrastructure
North Eastern Kenya's 38% penetration vs 94% in Nairobi represents a massive underserved market. Government-industry partnerships to expand 4G coverage could add 2–3M active users within 3 years.
🔴 High Priority
Fast-Track Interoperability
Full interoperability between M-Pesa, Airtel Money, and T-Kash would reduce friction for ~4M non-M-Pesa users and unlock cross-platform merchant payments — estimated at KES 800B in new transaction value.
🟡 Medium Priority
Invest in Merchant Payment Infrastructure
Merchant payments grew from 11% to 28% of volume in 5 years. Targeted subsidies on Lipa Na M-Pesa terminals for micro-merchants in rural markets could accelerate this shift further.
🟢 Strategic
Expand Cross-Border Remittance Corridors
Kenya's diaspora in the UK, USA, and Gulf states sends $4B+ annually. Only 6% flows through mobile money. Reducing fees on M-Pesa Global and expanding partnerships could capture $800M in additional annual flows.
08  ·  Conclusion

Kenya as a Global Blueprint

Kenya's mobile money story is not just a national success — it is a global template for financial inclusion through technology.

The data tells a clear story: mobile money in Kenya has moved from a utility for the unbanked to the central nervous system of the entire economy. With 87% adult adoption, transaction values exceeding half of GDP, and rapidly diversifying use cases, the platform has transcended its original purpose.

The next frontier — rural penetration, interoperability, cross-border expansion, and MSME credit — will require deliberate policy choices and continued private investment. The foundation, however, has never been stronger.
